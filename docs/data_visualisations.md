# Data visualisations

The RSC website contains a set of visualisations of aggregate statistics obtained from the RCGP RSC Database using the phenotypes in this collection.

These visualisations are primarily intended to help researchers quickly gauge the scale of data that is available. The definitions used do not necessarily precisely align with the measures that would be used in detailed resarch studies. No extra data cleaning is done beyond that performed before records are entered into the database, and thus anomalies may be apparent due to the inevitable presence of occasional coding errors.

For the purpose of the visualisation, the phenotypes can be divided into five groups

## 1. Acute conditions

For these we apply the phenotype with an observation window of one calendar year. To convert the total number of new cases to cases per 100 000 people, the denominator used is the total number of subjects who appear in the database during the year.

## 2. Chronic conditions

For these we apply the phenotype with a status date of the last day of the calendar year shown. To convert these to populations percentages, the denominator used is the total number of subjects present in the database.

## 3. Categorisations

As for chronic conditions, the denominator used is the total number of subjects present in the database. No age cutoffs are applied.

## 4. Drug/vaccine administrations or prescriptions

The data shown is the total number of people in the database that have been recorded as having at least one prescription for, or adminstration of, the drug or vaccine in the calendar year shown. As the definitions of target groups for different drugs and vaccines are complex, no attempt is made to represent these as take up percentages, etc. The raw count data is shown in table format only. 

## 5. Measurements

For simple measurements such as pulse rate, where the phenotype contains no element of categorisation, the value shown is the number of people for whom at least one measurement of the quantity has been recorded in the patient record in the year shown. The raw count data is shown in table format only.
